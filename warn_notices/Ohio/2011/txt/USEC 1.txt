The USEC facility is located on U.S. Route 23 in Pike County, approximately 25 miles north of the city of Portsmouth, Ohio. The plant address is P. 0. Box 628, 3930 U.S. Route 23 South, Piketon, Ohio 45661. It is anticipated that the reduction-in-force will affect more than 500 employees at the Piketon USEC facility. This will include salaried employees as well as employees who are represented by the United Steelworkers International Union, Local 689. USEC's work on its Cold Shutdown Contract with the Department of Energy (DOE) is to conclude by March 28, 2011. It is USEC's understanding that the Department of Energy's (DOE's) Decontamination and Decommissioning contractor, Fluor-B&W (FBP) will continue to perform some of the work currently performed by USEC GS under the Cold Shutdown Contract and that it intends to offer employment to USEC salaried and hourly represented employees to perform some work currently pe:rformed by USEC. The specific timing, number and terms of such offers are the responsibility of FBP and USEC is not able to make any representations regarding FBP plans at this time. FBP will provide information directly to the workers. Layoffs associated with the termination of the Cold Shutdown Contract will begin on March 28, 201I and are expected to be completed by April 11, 2011. The reductions will be permanent. 01/26/2011 WED 12:50 FAX 8972972 id!003/003 Ms. Alice Worrell January 25, 2011 Page2 Layoffs for the collective bargaining unit employees will be in accordance with the applicable Collective Bargaining Agreement provisions. The appropriate union official contact for the United Steelworkers International Union, Local 689, is Mr. Bobby Graff, President, Post Office Box 467, Piketon, Ohio 45661-0467.
