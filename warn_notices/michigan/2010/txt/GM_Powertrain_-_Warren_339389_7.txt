Specifically, it is anticipated that cessation of production operations encompassing the 4T65 transmissions produced and a volume reduction affecting the 6T70/75 transmissions at the Warren, Michigan Powertrain plant will commence on or about December 23, 2010. As a result of the cessation of 4T65-4 Speed, and a volume reduction of the 6T70ll5 6Speed, layoff will begin on January 3, 2011. The elimination of 4 Speed 151 shift and 6 Speed volume reductions will result in the permanent layoff of approximately 277 employees represented by the UAW International Union, Bob King, President, 8000 East Jefferson, Detroit, Michigan 48214. Employees laid off by this action may be eligible to receive State Unemployment Compensation and/or Supplemental Unemployment Benefits in accordance with Exhibit "D" to the Agreement between General Motors LLC and the UAW dated October 15, 2007. For hourly employees, bumping rights do exist under the local collective bargaining agreement. The currently anticipated hourly job classifications and the number of employees to be impacted are attached. Attachment -UAW classifications and the number of employees who will be laid off pursuant to the local Seniority Agreement. Any questions regarding this matter may be directed to: Ronald Arnold Personnel Director Powertrain-Warren 2350 Mound Road Warren, Michigan 48091 Sincerely, 	Phone: 586-298-8028 RECEIVEDINU/~Ronald Arnold OCT.2 5 2010 Personnel Director RAPIDRESPONSE GENERAL MOTORS LLC -WARREN POWERTRAIN
